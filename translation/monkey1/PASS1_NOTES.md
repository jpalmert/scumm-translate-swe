## Overview

## Translation Philosophy Applied

Following the glossary guidelines:
- **Tone over accuracy**: Prioritized humor and wit over literal translation

## Key Translation Decisions

### 1. "END/TIP/POINT" Wordplay (Insult #1)
**English**: "This is the END for you" → "And I've got a little TIP for you. Get the POINT?"  
**Swedish**: "Det här är SLUTET för dig" → "Och jag har ett litet TIPS åt dig. Fattar du UDDEN?"

**Rationale**: The Swedish "TIPS" (advice/tip) and "UDDEN" (point of sword/the point) preserves the double meaning perfectly. "Fattar du" (do you get it) mirrors the English "Get the POINT?"

### 2. "Dairy Farmer/Cow" (Insult #8 - THE iconic line)
**English**: "You fight like a dairy farmer" → "How appropriate. You fight like a cow."  
**Swedish**: "Du slåss som en mjölkbonde" → "Så passande. Du slåss som en ko."

**Rationale**: Direct translation works perfectly here. Used "Så passande" (not "Hur passande") as noted in pun_inventory.md. This is the most famous line in the game and the wordplay translates cleanly.

### 3. "Shish Kebab/Feather-duster" (Insult #2)
**English**: "Soon you'll be wearing my sword like a shish kebab!" → "First you'd better stop waving it like a feather-duster."  
**Swedish**: "Snart kommer du bära mitt svärd som en grillspett!" → "Först måste du sluta vifta med det som en dammvippa."

**Rationale**: "Grillspett" (grill skewer) captures the kebab imagery. "Dammvippa" (feather duster) preserves the "waving around ineffectively" mockery.

### 4. "Janitor Job" (Insult #3)
**English**: "My handkerchief will wipe up your blood!" → "So you got that job as janitor, after all."  
**Swedish**: "Min näsduk kommer torka upp ditt blod!" → "Så du fick det där jobbet som vaktmästare till slut."

**Rationale**: "Vaktmästare" (caretaker/janitor) - the comeback mocks the "wiping up" boast by referencing cleaning work. Added "till slut" (finally/after all) for natural Swedish flow.

### 5. "Breath Smell" (Insult #4)
**English**: "People fall at my feet when they see me coming." → "Even BEFORE they smell your breath?"  
**Swedish**: "Folk faller vid mina fötter när de ser mig komma." → "Även INNAN de känner lukten av din andedräkt?"

**Rationale**: Direct translation works. "Känner lukten av" (smell the scent of) is more natural Swedish than literal "luktar".

### 6. "Dog Teacher" (Insult #5)
**English**: "I once owned a dog that was smarter than you." → "He must have taught you everything you know."  
**Swedish**: "Jag ägde en gång en hund som var smartare än dig." → "Han måste ha lärt dig allt du kan."

**Rationale**: The comeback implies the opponent's stupidity by suggesting the smarter dog was their teacher. Clean translation.

### 7. "Already Puked" (Insult #6)
**English**: "You make me want to puke." → "You make me think somebody already did."  
**Swedish**: "Du får mig att vilja kräkas." → "Du får mig att tro att nån redan har gjort det."

**Rationale**: The comeback suggests the opponent IS vomit. "Nån" (someone) is appropriately casual/crude for pirate speech.

### 8. "Running Fast" (Insult #7)
**English**: "Nobody's ever drawn blood from me and nobody ever will!" → "You run THAT fast?"  
**Swedish**: "Ingen har nånsin dragit blod från mig och ingen kommer nånsin göra det!" → "Springer du SÅ fort?"

**Rationale**: Mockingly suggests cowardice - they avoid bloodshed by running away. Simple and effective.

### 9. "Nose Picking" (Insult #9)
**English**: "I got this scar on my face during a mighty struggle!" → "I hope now you've learned to stop picking your nose."  
**Swedish**: "Jag fick det här ärret i ansiktet under en väldig strid!" → "Jag hoppas att du nu lärt dig sluta peta näsan."

**Rationale**: Suggests the "mighty struggle" was just picking their nose too hard. Juvenile but funny insult.

### 10. "Borrow Diapers" (Insult #10)
**English**: "Have you stopped wearing diapers yet?" → "Why, did you want to borrow one?"  
**Swedish**: "Har du slutat använda blöjor än?" → "Varför, ville du låna en?"

**Rationale**: Classic reversal - implying the insulter needs diapers. Sharp comeback.

### 11. "Never Heard Of You" (Insult #11)
**English**: "I've heard you were a contemptible sneak." → "Too bad no one's ever heard of YOU at all."  
**Swedish**: "Jag har hört att du är ett föraktligt kryp." → "Synd att ingen nånsin hört talas om DIG överhuvudtaget."

**Rationale**: "Kryp" (creepy-crawly/vermin) for "contemptible sneak". The comeback suggests total insignificance - being talked about negatively beats anonymity.

### 12. "Using Your Brains" (Insult #12)
**English**: "You're no match for my brains, you poor fool." → "I'd be in real trouble if you ever used them."  
**Swedish**: "Du är ingen match för mitt förstånd, din stackars dåre." → "Då skulle jag vara i rejäla problem om du nånsin använde det."

**Rationale**: "Förstånd" (understanding/intelligence) instead of literal "brains". The comeback implies the boaster never uses their brain. "Rejäla problem" (real trouble) adds emphasis.

### 13. "Comfortable Beggar" (Insult #13)
**English**: "You have the manners of a beggar." → "I wanted to make sure you'd feel comfortable with me."  
**Swedish**: "Du har manérerna hos en tiggare." → "Jag ville vara säker på att du skulle känna dig bekväm med mig."

**Rationale**: Reversal - admitting poor manners to mock the insulter as a beggar. Works perfectly in Swedish.

### 14. "Hemorrhoids" (Insult #14)
**English**: "I'm not going to take your insolence sitting down!" → "Your hemorrhoids are flaring up again, eh?"  
**Swedish**: "Jag tänker inte tåla din oförskämdhet sittande!" → "Spelar dina hemorrojder upp igen?"

**Rationale**: Medical humor - suggests they can't sit because of hemorrhoids, not bravery. "Spelar upp" (acting up) is the natural Swedish expression.

### 15. "Never Learned Words" (Insult #15)
**English**: "There are no words for how disgusting you are." → "Yes there are. You just never learned them."  
**Swedish**: "Det finns inga ord för hur äcklig du är." → "Visst finns det. Du har bara aldrig lärt dig dem."

**Rationale**: Mockingly suggests the insulter is uneducated. "Visst" (certainly/sure) adds sarcastic emphasis.

### 16. "Family Reunion" (Insult #16)
**English**: "I've spoken with apes more polite than you." → "I'm glad to hear you attended your family reunion."  
**Swedish**: "Jag har talat med apor som är artigare än dig." → "Vad roligt att höra att du var på familjeträffen."

**Rationale**: Implies the insulter's family are apes. "Vad roligt" (how nice) is politely sarcastic. Perfect tone.

## Weak Generic Responses

4. **"Uhh... Could you repeat that? I didn't quite get it."** → **"Öhh... Kan du upprepa det? Jag hörde inte riktigt."** - Stalling/confusion

## Character Voice Consistency

All translations maintain:
- **Casual pirate register**: Using "nån" (someone), "nånsin" (ever), informal grammar
- **Sarcastic wit**: Sharp reversals and mockery in every comeback
- **Playful insults**: Not too crude, keeping the game's comedic tone
- **Swedish idioms**: "Spelar upp" (acting up), "Vad roligt" (how nice - sarcastic)

## Technical Format

All strings follow scummtr format:
```
[room:TYPE#resnum](opcode)Swedish text
```

Example: `[088:SCRP#0085](27)Du slåss som en mjölkbonde.`

- Room 088 = Pirate sword fighting area
- SCRP#0085 = Insults script
- SCRP#0086 = Comebacks script
- Opcode (27) = Dialog display code
- Order is critical - insult N must pair with comeback N
