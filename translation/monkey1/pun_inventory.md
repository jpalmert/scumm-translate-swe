# Language-Dependent Strings - Puns, Jokes & Wordplay

This document catalogs all strings that require creative translation rather than literal translation due to wordplay, cultural references, or language-specific humor.

## CRITICAL: Insult Swordfighting System

**This is a complete interconnected system - translate as a coherent set!**

The game's signature feature: pirates duel with insults and comebacks. Each insult has specific correct comebacks. The player learns insults/comebacks by fighting random pirates, then must use them correctly against the Sword Master.

### Training Examples (from Captain Smirk)
These teach the system:

**Insult:** "You fight like a dairy farmer."
**Correct Comeback:** "How appropriate. You fight like a cow."

**Insult:** "Soon you'll be wearing my sword like a shish-kabob!"  
**Correct Comeback:** "First you'd better stop waving it around like a feather-duster."

### Complete Insult/Comeback Pairs

**Standard Pirate Insults (player learns these):**

1. "This is the END for you, you gutter-crawling cur!"
   → "And I've got a little TIP for you. Get the POINT?"

2. "Soon you'll be wearing my sword like a shish kebab!"
   → "First you'd better stop waving it like a feather-duster."

3. "My handkerchief will wipe up your blood!"
   → "So you got that job as janitor, after all."

4. "People fall at my feet when they see me coming."
   → "Even BEFORE they smell your breath?"

5. "I once owned a dog that was smarter than you."
   → "He must have taught you everything you know."

6. "You make me want to puke."
   → "You make me think somebody already did."

7. "Nobody's ever drawn blood from me and nobody ever will!"
   → "You run THAT fast?"

8. "You fight like a dairy farmer."
   → "How appropriate. You fight like a cow."

9. "I got this scar on my face during a mighty struggle!"
   → "I hope now you've learned to stop picking your nose."

10. "Have you stopped wearing diapers yet?"
    → "Why, did you want to borrow one?"

11. "I've heard you were a contemptible sneak."
    → "Too bad no one's ever heard of YOU at all."

12. "You're no match for my brains, you poor fool."
    → "I'd be in real trouble if you ever used them."

13. "You have the manners of a beggar."
    → "I wanted to make sure you'd feel comfortable with me."

14. "I'm not going to take your insolence sitting down!"
    → "Your hemorrhoids are flaring up again, eh?"

15. "There are no words for how disgusting you are."
    → "Yes there are. You just never learned them."

16. "I've spoken with apes more polite than you."
    → "I'm glad to hear you attended your family reunion."

### Weak Generic Responses (wrong answers)
- "Oh yeah?"
- "I am rubber, you are glue."
- "I'm shaking, I'm shaking."
- "Uhh... Could you repeat that? I didn't quite get it."

### Translation Strategy for Insult Fighting

This system is THE MOST IMPORTANT translation challenge. Requirements:

1. **Insults must rhyme or have rhythm in Swedish** (if possible)
2. **Comebacks must be clever wordplay related to the insult**
3. **The pair relationship must be obvious** (so players can learn)
4. **Must remain pirate-themed**
5. **Keep similar offensiveness level** (not too rude, playfully insulting)

**Recommended approach:**
- Translate concept rather than words
- Swedish wordplay is different from English
- Consider Swedish insult traditions
- Test that comeback clearly responds to insult

**Example rework for Swedish:**

English: "You fight like a dairy farmer" → "How appropriate. You fight like a cow."

Swedish: "Du slåss som en mjölkbonde" → "Så passande. Du slåss som en ko."
(Note: "Så passande" not "Hur passande" - avoid literal translation errors. Direct translation works here, but may need creativity for others)

## Wordplay & Puns

### Line 69-80: Captain's Log
The captain's log from the ship contains the recipe joke:
"The directions we purchased on Mêlée proved to be a recipe, not a map as we had believed."

Later in game, the actual "map" to Monkey Island is discovered to be dancing lessons.

**Translation:** Preserve the misdirection. "Directions" works in Swedish as "anvisningar" which could be cooking OR navigation.

### Line 2637-2681: Captain Smirk's Sword Training
"Sword fighting is kinda like making love. It's not always what you do, but what you say."

Swedish equivalent needed - this metaphor must work culturally.

### Line 3500: Dancing Lessons as Map
"This is no map! It looks like... ...dancing lessons!"

The "dance steps" are: "Back! Two-three-four! Left! Two-three-four!"

These need to remain movement directions that could plausibly be mistaken for navigation.

### LOOM Advertisement (lines 4002-4008)
Complete advertisement for another LucasArts game. This is a famous fourth-wall-breaking joke.

**Keep in English** - it's a product ad and cultural reference. Maybe add translation in parentheses if needed.

### Lines 3012-3029: Idol Theft Explanations
Multiple excuses that all get validated by the Governor:
- "She said I could have it!" → "That's right. I did."
- "I was just going to borrow it!" → "That's right. I loaned it to him."
- "It belongs in a museum!" → "That's right. It does."

The joke is she supports whatever excuse. Translate maintaining the absurdist humor.
