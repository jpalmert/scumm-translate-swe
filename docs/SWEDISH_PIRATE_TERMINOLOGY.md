## Pirate Characterization in Swedish

### Speech Patterns

Swedish pirates in literature and film tend to:
- Use archaic or nautical terms even in casual conversation
- Mix in mild curses ("tusan", "förbaskat", "sjutton")
- Use compound words creatively
- Reference the sea in metaphors
- Drop articles sometimes (sailor's speech economy)
- Use "vid min ära" (by my honor), "vid himlens alla stjärnor" (by all stars in heaven)

### Tone Guidelines

**For standard pirates (Scumm Bar types):**
- Boastful but not overly formal
- Colorful language without being vulgar
- References to sailing, drinking, fighting
- Somewhat crude but good-natured

**For the Governor (Elaine Marley):**
- Educated, proper Swedish
- Firm but fair
- No sailor slang
- Uses subjunctive mood correctly

**For LeChuck:**
- Menacing but theatrical
- Old-fashioned expressions ("vid alla havets makter")
- Grand, sweeping threats
- Supernatural imagery

**For Guybrush:**
- Modern, casual Swedish
- Slightly sarcastic
- Self-deprecating humor
- References things pirates wouldn't know (breaks fourth wall)

## Comparative Examples

**English:** "Shiver me timbers!"  
**Swedish:** "Blixt och dunder!" or "Tusan också!" (no direct equivalent)

**English:** "Avast, ye scurvy dogs!"  
**Swedish:** "Stopp där, era sjöräddor!" or "Håll där, era uslingarl!"

**English:** "Yo ho ho and a bottle of rum!"  
**Swedish:** "Yo ho ho och en flaska rom!" (direct translation works)

**English:** "Dead men tell no tales."  
**Swedish:** "Döda män sjunger inga visor." (Dead men sing no songs - more poetic)

**English:** "Pieces of eight!"  
**Swedish:** "Dubloner!" (doubloons - evokes pirates better than literal translation)

**English:** "Landlubber!"  
**Swedish:** "Lantkrabba!" (land-crab)

**English:** "Walk the plank!"  
**Swedish:** "Gå plankan!"

## Translation Strategy for Monkey Island

1. **Prioritize entertainment over historical accuracy** - Use terms that Swedish audiences associate with pirates from popular culture

2. **Mix archaic and modern** - Like the English original, blend old nautical terms with modern sensibilities

3. **Preserve the tone** - The game is a comedy that parodies pirate tropes. Swedish version should feel equally playful

4. **Use Kapten Haddock as inspiration** - Swedish readers know Tintin's Kapten Haddock and his colorful curses as the gold standard for nautical exclamations

5. **Avoid overused terms** - Don't use "skurk" for every villain. Mix it up: drummel, slyngel, usling, bov, rackare

6. **Compound words** - Swedish allows creative compounds: "havsbusare" (sea rascal), "vågskvalpare" (wave-splasher), "sjöskumt" (sea scum)

7. **Regional flavor** - Western Swedish (Gothenburg) has strong maritime traditions. Some western dialect words can add authenticity

8. **Modern touches** - Guybrush can use modern slang to contrast with the pirate-era setting (part of the humor)
